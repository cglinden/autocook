Year 2000 Status

Cook does not suffer from Year 2000 problems.
  • Cook stores dates internally in Unix style (i.e. seconds offset), so internal storage of times and dates does not suffer from any Y2K problems.
  • Cook always uses the ANSI C standard strftime function to display times and dates. (This assumes that your vendor has supplied a compliant strftime.) This means that displaying dates does not assume fixed field widths, nor will it display the year 2000 as ``100''.
  • When a user enters dates, they may specify years with an explicit century. If a user enters a year with an implicit century, the closest is assumed.
